Overview

Volleyball is a team sport played with one ball and a middle net. One team try to hit the ball over the net, land it on the opponent's court. Another team must send the ball back over the net within 3 tries, without letting it touch the ground. A player is not allowed to hit the ball twice consecutively except when attempting a block.

The ball is put into the play when the right back-row player serve it to the other team. The game keeps going until the ball touches the ground, or goes out the court, or a team fails to return it to other side, or commits a fault.

When to get a point?

The team winning a rally scores a point. When the receiving team wins a rally, it gains a point and the right to serve, and its players rotate one position clockwise.

Who is the winner?

  • The best of three or best of five games will win matches.
  • In a non-deciding game, the team first scores 25 points with a minimum two-point advantage.
  • In a deciding game, team that first scores 15 points with a minimum two-point advantage.

Brief history

The sport originally invented in the United States, and is now popular all other the world. It ranks behind only soccer among participation sports. There are two kinds of competitive volleyball. Team volleyball is played indoors on a hard court with 6 people per team. Beach volleyball is played outdoors on the sand with 2 players per team.
